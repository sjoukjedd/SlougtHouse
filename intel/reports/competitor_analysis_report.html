Unique position confirmed

No other wearable on the market combines ECG and ICG simultaneously in an ambulatory form factor with a mobile app interface. The VU-AMS occupies this space alone. ICG-derived cardiac output, stroke volume, and baroreflex sensitivity are unavailable from any consumer or research wearable competitor.

⚠️
Primary risk: signal quality perception, not signal capability

If the research community accepts PPG-derived cardiac output estimates (pulse transit time methods) as a substitute for ICG, the moat erodes. This is a literature-watch priority for Vasquez.

🔴
Regulatory gap

Hexoskin (FDA-cleared) and Vivalink (FDA-cleared) have cleared regulatory hurdles VU-AMS has not. For US clinical research contracts this is a material disadvantage.

Key Findings & Recommendations
🔬
Finding 1 — ICG moat is real and currently uncontested in wearables

No wearable competitor measures ICG. MindWare Mobile ICG is the only comparable system and is not field-grade. VU-AMS uniquely enables ambulatory cardiac output, stroke volume, and baroreflex sensitivity — the key hemodynamic stress markers.

📱
Finding 2 — Mobile iOS app is a genuine differentiator in the research segment

No research competitor (MindWare, BIOPAC) has a mobile-first interface. Consumer competitors have apps but no clinical depth. The combination of research-grade signal quality with a modern iOS interface is unique to VU-AMS.

⚡
Finding 3 — EDA is the highest-value sensor gap

Empatica's EDA measurement captures sympathetic nervous system activity — the other half of the autonomic stress response. Adding EDA to VU-AMS would make it the only device covering both parasympathetic (HRV via ECG) and sympathetic (EDA) branches simultaneously. Study 001 (Vasquez) should evaluate chest-EDA feasibility as priority 1.

⚠️
Finding 4 — Regulatory gap is a commercial risk

Hexoskin and Vivalink hold FDA clearance. VU-AMS does not. For US-market clinical research contracts, hospital partnerships, and insurance reimbursement, this is a material gap. A regulatory strategy should be defined before the next hardware generation is finalised.

🔭
Finding 5 — Watch pulse transit time literature (medium-term threat)

Several consumer and clinical companies are developing PPG + ECG based cardiac output estimation via pulse transit time (PTT). If this approach gains research acceptance as a substitute for ICG, the moat narrows significantly. Vasquez to monitor: Caretaker VitalStream PTT validation studies, SOMNOtouch PTT publications, and any meta-analyses comparing PTT-CO vs ICG-CO in ambulatory settings.
