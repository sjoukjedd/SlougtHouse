Critical outcomes from Meeting 001
  • ! Safety blocker identified: The USB-C recording interlock required by IEC 60601-1 Type BF is not implemented. Firmware must block recording when PIN_USB_DETECT is high. No human subject recordings permitted until ACT-004 ships.
  • 1 Critical path is SPI bus congestion: The SD card sharing SPI2_HOST with the ADC and IMU creates a throughput bottleneck. Migrating SD to SDMMC native 4-bit mode (GPIO35–40) unblocks raw ICG waveform storage (Z-block), which in turn unblocks Vasquez's full algorithm pipeline and VU-DAMS validation.
  • 2 Battery budget is razor-thin: Nair's back-of-envelope estimate puts average current draw at 115–120 mA against a 3400 mAh cell — only 28.3 hours against the 28-hour requirement. The AD5933 at 15 mA is the outlier; duty-cycling it to standby between 100 Hz polls could save ~14 mA average and move the margin to comfortable.
  • 3 PEP measurement has a systematic bias: The I-block records R-to-B interval, not Q-to-B. True PEP requires Q-wave detection, adding 30–50 ms. The bias is constant within-subject so stress tracking is unaffected, but absolute comparisons against published normative data will show an offset.
  • 4 Four iOS integration tests are blocked pending a simulator peripheral app that does not yet exist. Chen owns ACT-015; this is the single biggest blocker to iOS test coverage.
  • 5 CI pipeline does not exist. All 25 tests in TP-001 are manual-only. Bell cannot run tests consistently without automated execution. Kim's ACT-025 is a force-multiplier on every other improvement — it is how we know fixes actually hold.

Top outcomes — New Horizons Meeting 001
  • 1 Three concepts selected for active development: BRAVO (BRS algorithm extension), FIELDWATCH (occupational SaaS service), and VU-AMS PATCH (adhesive chest patch form factor). Voted in Phase 3. All others remain in the idea log.
  • 2 BRAVO is the fastest path to scientific differentiation. Baroreflex sensitivity via the Sequence Method requires no new hardware — just Vasquez's algorithm and ~2 months of combined work. Positions the VU-AMS as the only wearable with simultaneous BRS, HRV, and haemodynamic output.
  • 3 FIELDWATCH unlocks the biggest near-term commercial opportunity. The B2B occupational health market is using smartwatches and getting unreliable data. A managed service with VU-AMS-grade ICG metrics at premium price point has no real competitor. Requires cloud pipeline (Kim + Reyes) and Protocol Builder (Quinn + Chen).
  • ~ VU-AMS PATCH is the largest bet. Rigid-flex redesign into a cable-free adhesive chest patch opens neonatal, paediatric, and post-operative markets. But it is 9–12 months of parallel hardware, firmware, and industrial design work. Critical unknown: dry-electrode ICG performance in patch geometry. Nair's feasibility memo in 6 weeks is the go/no-go gate.
  • 4 Regulatory strategy is clear: All three selected concepts should be positioned as Research Use Only for the next 18 months. No clinical diagnostic claims. Existing IEC 60601-1 Type BF certification covers the hardware. Wolff confirmed.
