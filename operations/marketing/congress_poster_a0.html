Background & Rationale

Why ICG in a wearable matters.

Ambulatory stress research requires access to haemodynamic parameters — cardiac output (CO), stroke volume (SV), pre-ejection period (PEP) and baroreflex sensitivity (BRS) — that cannot be derived from ECG, PPG or actigraphy alone. Impedance Cardiography (ICG) provides non-invasive continuous access to these parameters but has historically been confined to laboratory settings.

The VU-AMS brings research-grade ICG into daily-life contexts: the workplace, sleep laboratory, clinical protocol, or field study. No other ambulatory wearable currently combines ICG with ECG in a mobile-app-enabled platform.

Research Applications
Occupational Stress
Continuous monitoring of cardiac and sympathetic stress responses in naturalistic work environments. 28h protocol support.
Psychophysiology Research
Full autonomic profiling: parasympathetic (HRV/BRS) + sympathetic (EDA) + haemodynamic (CO/SV) simultaneously.

Ambulatory haemodynamic assessment in patient populations. Validated ICG alongside clinical reference methods.
