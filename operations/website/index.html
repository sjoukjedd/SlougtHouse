The Device

Research-grade sensing,
built for the real world.

The VU-AMS is a 55 × 55 mm chest-worn device that captures the full physiological signature of stress — from beat-to-beat haemodynamics to skin conductance — without restricting daily activity.

ECG + ICG Precision

Simultaneous 2-lead ECG and 4-electrode Impedance Cardiography at 1 kHz with 24-bit resolution. The only ambulatory wearable that delivers cardiac output and stroke volume in the field — not derivable from any other wearable signal.

24-bit · 1 kHz · CMRR >120 dB
28-Hour Battery Life

Dual 1000 mAh Li-ion cells power the ESP32-S3 platform and all sensor subsystems for a full day's ambulatory study — plus overnight. Mechanical USB-C cover ensures IEC 60601-1 BF-classified patient safety during continuous wear.

28 h typical · IEC 60601-1 BF
Mobile + Desktop Analysis

Live BLE 5.0 streaming to iPhone, iPad, and Apple Watch for real-time signal display and electrode contact monitoring. VU-DAMS Java desktop application handles offline artefact rejection, ensemble averaging, and export to SPSS, MATLAB, and R.

iOS · watchOS · VU-DAMS
Multi-Signal Fusion

Beyond ECG and ICG: PPG/SpO₂ cross-validation, skin conductance (SCL/EDA) for sympathetic arousal, TMP117 body temperature, 9-DOF inertial sensing for posture and activity classification, and barometric pressure for environmental context.

7 sensor modalities

The Science

Haemodynamics without the hospital.

Impedance Cardiography (ICG) is a non-invasive technique that uses a low-level alternating current (1 mA rms at 32 kHz) to measure changes in thoracic electrical impedance synchronised with the cardiac cycle. From the impedance waveform and its derivative (dZ/dt), the VU-AMS extracts beat-to-beat stroke volume, cardiac output, and the pre-ejection period — a direct index of sympathetic contractility.

Heart Rate Variability (HRV) reflects the autonomic nervous system's continuous modulation of heart rate. The parasympathetic branch drives high-frequency variability; the sympathetic branch modulates lower frequencies. The VU-AMS resolves both: time-domain indices (SDNN, RMSSD), frequency-domain power spectra (LF 0.04–0.15 Hz; HF 0.15–0.4 Hz), and non-linear measures (SD1/SD2, approximate entropy).

Baroreflex Sensitivity (BRS) — the relationship between beat-to-beat blood pressure and R-R interval — quantifies autonomic balance and cardiovascular reactivity. Derived from the cross-spectral analysis of ICG-derived cardiac output and ECG-derived HRV, it is uniquely available in an ambulatory setting only through the VU-AMS.
